Semilatticesüîó

A semilattice is a partially ordered set where all elements have a meet (a meet semilattice), or a join (a join semilattice). As is typical with algebraic structures, we define a semilattice homomorphism as being a map which commutes with the binary operator. Since being a semilattice is a property of (A,‚àß)(A, \land)(A,‚àß), we have a characterisation of equality of semilattices: Two semilattices are equal precisely when their underlying types are equivalent by some homomorphic equivalence.
